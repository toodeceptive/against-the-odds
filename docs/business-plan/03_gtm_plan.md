# Against The Odds — Go-to-Market Plan

## Explicit assumptions (adjust as facts change)

- **Brand / site**: Against The Odds (AO), Shopify store at `aodrop.com`.
- **Category**: Premium streetwear / athleisure basics (starting with hoodies; later hats/tees).
- **Launch motion**: “Drop” model (limited run) to create urgency and reduce inventory risk.
- **Audience**: US-first, English-first.
- **Fulfillment**: One shipping origin (initially) with standard + expedited options.
- **Tooling**: Shopify + Shopify Email or Klaviyo for flows; Shopify analytics + GA4; creator tracking via discount codes + UTMs.
- **Budget**: $0–$2k in month 1 for seeding/creator samples/tools; paid ads optional and not required for initial validation.
- **Capacity**: Solo/founder-led ops with light contractor support (design/video editing) if needed.

## Positioning summary

### One-liner

**Premium essentials for people who keep going—built for the ones who live “against the odds.”**

### What we sell (in plain English)

AO sells **high-quality, wearable statement pieces** (starting with hoodies) that signal resilience and ambition without feeling gimmicky.

### Why it wins

- **Story + identity**: Customers aren’t just buying fabric—they’re buying a badge (resilience, grit, self-belief).
- **Quality-first**: Fit, weight, stitching, and print/embroidery quality are the product (not just the logo).
- **Drop energy**: Limited releases create urgency and community moments without large ad spend.

### Positioning statement

For people who are building, training, grinding, or rebuilding, **Against The Odds** is the apparel brand that turns personal struggle into visible confidence—**premium basics that feel earned**, not trendy.

### Proof points to emphasize (once validated)

- Fabric weight (gsm/oz), construction details, fit notes
- Durability testing / wash results
- Behind-the-scenes manufacturing + QC
- Real customer stories (UGC + testimonials)

## Target segments + messaging angles

We’ll focus on segments where identity, motivation, and “earned confidence” are already culturally strong.

### Segment A — Builders (founders, creators, career grinders)

- **Job to be done**: feel focused + confident; signal “I’m working on something”.
- **Hook**: “Quiet confidence for people building in silence.”
- **Content angles**: routines, late nights, process > outcome, setbacks.
- **Offer framing**: limited drop, founder story, quality details.
- **Objections**: “It’s just another hoodie.” Answer with materials, fit, and story.

### Segment B — Fitness / training community

- **Job to be done**: comfort + durability; signal discipline.
- **Hook**: “Train like it’s personal.” / “Show up anyway.”
- **Content angles**: gym fits, warmups, PR attempts, recovery, consistency.
- **Offer framing**: heavyweight warmth, movement comfort, holds shape.
- **Objections**: “Will it shrink? Will it hold up?” Answer with specs + wash tests.

### Segment C — Streetwear customers (quality + drop culture)

- **Job to be done**: premium basics that look right with everything; scarcity matters.
- **Hook**: “A drop you can actually wear every day.”
- **Content angles**: fit checks, details, embroidery/print closeups, styling reels.
- **Offer framing**: limited run, numbered run (optional), restock policy clarity.
- **Objections**: “Why buy this over [brand]?” Answer with value + story.

### Segment D — Supporters / gifters (friends/family of grinders)

- **Job to be done**: meaningful gift that says “I see you.”
- **Hook**: “Gift grit.”
- **Content angles**: gift moments, story captions, “for someone who…” lists.
- **Offer framing**: gift-ready packaging, easy returns/exchanges.

## Channel strategy

### Organic content (TikTok/IG/YouTube Shorts)

**Goal**: generate attention + trust at low cost; convert to email waitlist + early access.

**Operating system**

- **Cadence (minimum viable)**: 5 short-form posts/week + 2 stories/day + 1 longer-form (YouTube) every 2 weeks.
- **Repurposing**: one core video → cutdowns for TikTok/IG/Shorts; still frames for Pinterest (optional).
- **CTA hierarchy**:
  1. “Join the drop list” (email capture) pre-launch
  2. “Early access link” during launch week
  3. “Shop the drop” post-launch

**Content pillars (rotate weekly)**

- **P1 — Brand story**: origin, meaning of “Against The Odds,” founder POV.
- **P2 — Product truth**: fabric/fit, details, comparisons, wash tests.
- **P3 — Proof**: UGC, testimonials, creator try-ons, social proof snippets.
- **P4 — Lifestyle**: gym/work routines, “day in the life,” motivational micro-stories.
- **P5 — Drop energy**: countdowns, behind-the-scenes, packing orders, sold-out updates.

**Hook bank (examples)**

- “If you’re building in silence, this is for you.”
- “The hoodie I wear when I’m not feeling confident… but I show up anyway.”
- “Most brands hide manufacturing—here’s ours.”
- “Heavyweight hoodie test: does it keep its shape after 10 washes?”

**On-platform CTAs**

- Link-in-bio to a single landing page (waitlist → drop page).
- Comment keyword automation optional (manual replies OK at first): “Comment ‘ODDS’ and I’ll DM early access.”

### Creators / affiliates

**Goal**: borrow trust + reach with micro-creators; generate UGC assets; drive trackable sales.

**Creator profile**

- Micro-creators: **10k–250k** followers
- Niches: fitness, “discipline” content, entrepreneur grindset, streetwear try-ons, motivational storytelling
- Selection criteria: high comment quality, consistent posting, camera comfort, aligns with resilience tone

**Offer structure (simple + scalable)**

- **Seeding**: free product + shipping for content (no guarantees for smallest creators).
- **Affiliate**: 10–15% commission (or flat $10/order) via unique discount code.
- **Performance bonus**: tiered bonus at 10 / 25 / 50 orders.

**Tracking + attribution**

- Unique creator code: `AO_<NAME>` (e.g., `AO_MAYA`)
- UTM template: `?utm_source=creator&utm_medium=ig_reel&utm_campaign=drop1&utm_content=<creator_handle>`
- Weekly performance sheet: clicks, orders, revenue, CPA (estimated), top creatives

**Process**

- Build a list of 50 creators → outreach 10/day for 5 days
- Send a 1-page brief: brand story, product details, do/don’t, posting window, CTA copy
- Require **2 assets minimum**: 1 try-on + 1 story sequence (or 1 TikTok + 1 IG Reel)
- Repost rights (whitelisting optional later)

### Email/SMS

**Goal**: own demand, convert on launch, recover abandoned sessions, and build repeat purchase.

**List building**

- Pre-launch waitlist with incentive (choose one):
  - Early access (preferred)
  - Free shipping for first 48 hours
  - “Drop 1” limited colorway access
- On-site capture placement: header bar, exit intent, product page modal, checkout opt-in.

**Flows (minimum set before launch)**

- **Welcome series (3 emails)**: story → product truth → early access/call to action
- **Abandoned cart (3)**: reminder → objections (fit/quality/shipping) → urgency
- **Browse abandon (2)**: viewed products + social proof
- **Post-purchase (3)**: thank you + care instructions → UGC request → next drop tease
- **Winback (2)**: 30/60 days depending on cadence (optional early)

**Campaigns**

- Pre-launch: 1x/week (build story + anticipation)
- Launch week: 3–5 emails (early access, live, low stock, last call)
- Post-launch: 1x/week (UGC, restock/news, community highlights)

### Paid (optional)

**Not required for Drop 1.** If used, keep it simple:

- **Retargeting only** (site visitors + email engagers) with UGC-style creative.
- KPI: \(ROAS\) is noisy early; focus on CPA and contribution margin.

## Launch campaign plan

### Pre-launch

**T-28 to T-15 (build list + trust)**

- Publish brand story page + founder narrative.
- Start daily story posting; 5 short-form posts/week.
- Build waitlist landing page and test email capture.
- Seed 10–20 creators to collect UGC before launch.
- Capture “product truth” assets: closeups, fit, weight, wash test clips.

**T-14 to T-8 (product education + social proof)**

- Fit checks + “why this hoodie is different” series (3 posts).
- UGC drip: 3–5 creator clips posted across week.
- Email: welcome sequence live; weekly pre-launch newsletter begins.

**T-7 to T-1 (countdown + conversion readiness)**

- Hard countdown daily (stories + 3 posts).
- Early access email: “Drop opens to list first” (24 hours).
- Finalize creator posting schedule (launch day + 48h).
- QA the store checkout, shipping rates, policies, and order notifications.

### Launch week

**Launch week schedule (example)**

- **Day -1**: Early access to waitlist (email + SMS optional). Creators tease “tomorrow”.
- **Day 0 (Launch)**:
  - Email 1: “Drop is live” (morning)
  - Post: hero product + story hook
  - Stories: FAQ stickers, sizing, shipping, limited inventory
  - Creator wave 1 posts
- **Day 1–2**: “Top questions answered” + UGC montage + “low stock” if true.
- **Day 3–5**: Community highlights + “how it fits” + “packing orders” content.
- **Day 6–7**: “Last call” or “closing drop” (only if actually closing).

### Post-launch

**Weeks 2–4 (retain + learn + prep next drop)**

- Publish “Drop 1 recap” with transparent numbers (optional, but builds trust).
- Post-purchase email pushes UGC and reviews.
- Identify top creator content; ask for 2nd round or ongoing affiliate.
- Run a customer interview sprint (5–10 customers): why bought, hesitations, what to improve.
- Plan Drop 2 based on learnings: colors, sizing, price, story angles.

## KPIs + instrumentation

### North-star (first 30 days)

- **Revenue**: total sales for Drop 1
- **Contribution margin** (estimated): after COGS + shipping subsidies + creator costs

### Leading indicators (weekly)

- **Email waitlist size** and **growth rate**
- **Organic reach**: views, watch time, saves, shares
- **Site sessions** (Shopify + GA4) and **landing page CVR to email**

### Conversion metrics (launch week)

- **Store conversion rate (CVR)**: sessions → purchases
- **AOV**: average order value
- **Checkout abandonment rate**
- **Top traffic sources** (organic / creator / email)

### Retention / proof (weeks 2–4)

- **Repeat purchase rate** (if applicable this early)
- **UGC rate**: % of orders that submit photo/video or tag
- **Review rate**: % of orders leaving a review

### Target ranges (based on small-drop, organic-first assumptions)

- **Waitlist**: 300–1,500 emails pre-launch (depends on posting consistency + creator seeding)
- **Launch week CVR**: 1.5%–3.0%
- **AOV**: $70–$110 (hoodie-led carts)
- **Email revenue share**: 25%–45% (typical when list is warmed)

### Instrumentation notes

- Use **UTMs** on every link (email, creators, bio link).
- Use **unique discount codes** per creator for attribution in Shopify.
- Maintain a weekly KPI snapshot in `docs/launch/04_analytics_and_kpis.md` (source of truth).
